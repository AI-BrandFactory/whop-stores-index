I cataloged 512 Whop stores across 20 niches in one weekend using Brave Search. The patterns that emerged are the cheapest market research any founder will ever do for the question "should I launch a productized community in 2026."

This playbook has two layers. The build guide walks you from a blank niche pick to your first 50 paying members in 60 days. Nine parts. Real store URLs throughout. Built to be opened by a solo founder on a Saturday morning and turned into a launch plan by Sunday night.

## Part 1: The Whop Economy in 2026

If you're reading this and you're not sure what Whop is, here's the 90-second version.

Whop is a platform for selling access to paid communities, paid content, and paid services. It started as a marketplace for trading-signal Discords in 2021, hit traction with crypto and sports-betting groups in 2022 and 2023, then quietly became the default surface for productized communities by mid-2024.

By 2026, Whop is what Gumroad was for ebooks in 2018, except for memberships. The whole stack is bundled. You get the storefront, the payments, the access gating to your Discord or Telegram, the affiliate system, the refund handling, the receipts. You don't have to wire Stripe to a custom membership site. You don't have to write Discord bot code to gate channels. You set a price, you point to your community, and Whop handles the rest.

The price floor is free. Yes, free. There's a real free-tier pattern where creators set up a free Whop and use it as a funnel to a paid one. Inside the index, see whop.com/c (The TradingHub Community Free) and whop.com/the-crypto-world-free for working examples.

The price ceiling is fuzzy but practically caps around $5,000 to $10,000 for one-time purchases and $1,000+/month for premium masterminds. There are higher tiers, but the market thins out fast above $1K/month. Most paying customers cap their willingness at the price of a low-end coaching program (~$500/month) or a meaningful course ($300 to $1,500 one-time).

What can you sell on Whop?

- Discord/Telegram access (the original use case, still the most common)
- Recorded courses (video + PDF, hosted natively or linked out)
- Live cohort coaching (pricing tiered, capped seat count)
- Trading or betting signals (often a feed inside Discord)
- Software access (signal bots, indicators, scrapers, prompt packs)
- 1-on-1 coaching slots (sold like a service product)
- Newsletter access (smaller use case but it exists)
- Hybrid bundles of any of the above

Why do founders pick Whop over Skool, Circle, or a Discord+Stripe duct-tape build?

Three reasons, and they show up over and over in the data.

**Speed.** A new operator can stand up a Whop store in an evening. Skool takes a couple days minimum, and a Discord+Stripe build is a weekend project at best. For someone testing an offer, Whop wins on time-to-first-customer.

**Discovery.** Whop's marketplace funnels real organic traffic to small stores. Skool has a feed but it's still creator-driven. Circle has zero discovery layer. Whop's marketplace is closer to the App Store, which means a tiny store can pick up its first 10 customers without paid ads.

**Payment plumbing.** Subscriptions, refunds, affiliates, country-specific pricing, taxes. All handled. A Discord+Stripe stack means you're writing webhooks and handling failed payments yourself. On Whop, that's the platform's problem.

The downside? You don't own the relationship the same way. If Whop changes the marketplace algorithm or fee structure, you're exposed. Most operators in the data treat Whop as the storefront, but pull the audience into Discord, Telegram, or email so they own the channel even if Whop changes the rules.

There's a deeper analysis of platform-versus-direct tradeoffs at aibrandfactory.com.

## Part 2: How to Build Your Own Whop Store

This is the 8-step build sequence. If you run it cleanly, you go from blank slate to a live store with first paying members inside 30 days. The data side of this playbook (Parts 3 through 8) is the reference you check at each step. The 500-store index is the comparison set you anchor against.

### Step 1: Pick the Niche (the 5-Question Filter)

Don't pick the biggest niche. Pick the one you can actually ship in. Run your candidate niche through these 5 questions. You need 5 yeses or pick again.

1. Do you have 90 minutes a week of original content in this niche? Not "could I research it." Already-in-your-head content. If no, the cadence will kill you by month 2.
2. Can you point to a real outcome a buyer gets? "Pass prop firm phase 1." "Lose 10 pounds in 60 days." "Land your first agency client." Vague outcomes price low and churn fast.
3. Is the niche already on Whop? Check Part 5 (the 20-niche map). If a niche has zero stores in the index, that's not opportunity, that's absence of demand. Pick a niche with at least 10 stores in the index.
4. Can you charge $19 minimum without flinching? If your honest answer is "I'd feel weird charging," you don't have enough proof yet. Build the proof first (free cohort, case studies) before launching paid.
5. Can you ship a v0 in 7 days? The store, the first 4 weeks of content, the welcome flow. If it would take you 60 days to even start, the niche is too big or the offer is too complex.

If you got 5 yeses, you have a niche. Move to step 2.

### Step 2: Pre-Launch Positioning

Three artifacts before you touch the Whop dashboard.

- **The name.** Two-word maximum if possible. Identity-led ("The Higher Brotherhood," "Discipline Circle") or outcome-led ("The Prop Firm Protocol"). Look at the standouts in your niche from Part 5 and pattern-match.
- **The 1-line promise.** "I help X get Y in Z time without W." Write it. Read it out loud. If you can't say it without qualifications, it's not tight enough.
- **The free funnel piece.** One short artifact you give away to prove competence: a 5-page PDF, a 12-minute YouTube video, a single email teardown. This is the top of your funnel. Without it, you're cold-DMing your first 50 members.

KPI for step 2: name registered, promise written down, free piece live somewhere public.

### Step 3: Whop Store Setup

The mechanical build. Plan for one focused afternoon.

- Register the store at whop.com. Connect Stripe. Verify the payout method.
- Brand kit. Logo (square, 512x512 minimum). Banner (1500x500). 3 brand colors. Get these done in Canva or Figma in under an hour, don't make this the bottleneck.
- About page. 4 sections: who this is for (the buyer profile in 2 lines), what you get (the deliverables list), why me (the trust signal from Part 6 step 5), the FAQ (5 to 7 entries answering the actual questions buyers ask).
- The 3-tier structure. This is the pattern almost every winning store on the index runs.
  - **Free tier.** The funnel. Captures email + Discord access. Smallest possible piece of value.
  - **Commitment tier ($19 to $97/month).** The main offer. Where most members live.
  - **Outcome tier ($197 to $497/month, or one-time $497 to $1,497).** The premium upsell. Live calls, 1-on-1 access, or done-for-you components.

Reference: whop.com/wealth runs $7.99 / $28.88 / lifetime. whop.com/source-trader runs $17.99 alerts / $69.99 day trading. Pick the closest 3 stores in your niche and copy their tier shape, then adjust.

### Step 4: Price Your Offer (Anchor Against the Index)

The 500-store index is your comparison set. Don't price by feel. Price by data.

- Open the index file. Filter to your niche.
- Pull the 10 nearest competitors. Note their entry tier price and their main tier price.
- Find the median. That's your reality check.
- The $19 floor exists because anything cheaper trains buyers to treat you as disposable. Do not price below $19/month except as a free funnel.
- The $97 sweet spot is where most buyers convert without too much thought. If your offer is course-only or community-only, this is your default.
- The $497 cap is where pricing stops being intuitive and starts requiring proof. If you can't show a case study, a track record, or a credibility signal, don't price above $497 yet.

If your offer is genuinely better than the median in your niche, price 10% above the median. If you're new and unproven, price at the median to compete on offer clarity, not price.

### Step 5: Content Cadence (The 90-Minute Weekly Practice)

Retention dies when cadence dies. Pick the rhythm before you launch and run it without missing.

- **Daily.** Required for signals stores and active communities. One post per day in Discord, even if it's a one-line check-in. Compounds trust over weeks.
- **Weekly.** Required for everyone. One substantial deliverable: a live call, a deep teardown, a watchlist, a themed challenge. This is your retention anchor.
- **Monthly.** Recommended. A bigger event: a guest interview, a cohort kickoff, a member spotlight, a wins recap.

The 90-minute weekly practice is the hard floor. Block 90 minutes on the same day every week. That's where you ship the weekly deliverable. If you can't commit to 90 minutes a week for the next 6 months, you don't have a Whop store, you have a side project.

### Step 6: Launch Tactics (First 50 Members)

Cold launches die. Warm launches close. Here's the warm playbook.

- **Existing audience first.** Email list, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube. Even 200 followers can produce 10 paying members if the offer is clear. If you have zero audience, you need to build the free funnel piece first and grow it for 30 days before launching paid.
- **Reddit and niche communities.** Find the 5 subreddits or Discord servers where your buyer hangs out. Don't spam. Contribute for 2 weeks, then post your launch when relevant.
- **Twitter/X.** A launch thread, then 14 days of daily posts about the offer (case studies, behind-the-scenes, member wins). The reach compounds.
- **The "free first 10" tactic.** Give the first 10 members free access in exchange for honest reviews and testimonials within 30 days. Use those testimonials on the storefront. This single move pays back more than any other launch tactic.
- **Founding member pricing.** Members 11 through 25 get 50% off for life. Standard Whop launch tactic. Closes the next wave fast.
- **Direct DMs.** DM 50 likely buyers personally. Awkward, effective, and the highest-converting channel for the first 50 members.

KPI: 50 paying members and 5 testimonials by end of launch month.

### Step 7: Retention Setup (the 30/60/90 Cadence)

Acquisition is fun. Retention compounds. Build these from day one.

- **Week 1 onboarding sequence.** Welcome DM, intro thread post, week-1 starter kit. New members should know exactly what to do in their first 72 hours. Most churn happens in week 1 because new members don't know where to start.
- **Month 2 cohort moment.** A live event, challenge, or cohort kickoff that recurring members get for free. Gives them a reason to renew at month 2 instead of churning.
- **Quarter 3 anniversary.** A member spotlight, a milestone gift, or a private session for members who've stayed 90+ days. The signal that you notice tenure.
- **Churn signals to watch.** Members who haven't posted in 14 days. Members who skipped the last 2 live calls. Members who downgraded a tier. Trigger a personal DM, not a generic email.
- **Win-back DMs.** When someone cancels, a single personal DM within 24 hours asking what didn't work. Recovers 10 to 20% of churns and gives you the feedback to fix the offer.

Industry benchmark on Whop is 50 to 70% month-2 retention. Below 50, the offer is broken. Above 80, raise the price.

### Step 8: Scaling (When to Add Tiers, When to Spin Out)

Don't scale until the base is stable. The signs you're ready:

- **Add a second tier** when the entry tier is consistently full and at least 20% of members are asking for "more." That's the demand signal for an outcome tier. Price it 3 to 5x the entry tier.
- **Spin out a sister store** when you've maxed out a niche sub-segment and there's an adjacent demographic the current brand can't reach. Example: a discipline brand for young men spins out a sister brand for women in their 30s.
- **Move from solo to small team** when content cadence is consistently slipping past your 90-minute weekly block. Hire a community manager first (Discord moderation, member onboarding), then a content assistant, then a sales person if you're running outbound.

Don't scale by buying ads in month 2. The data in Part 7 shows the highest-LTV stores all built organic before they touched paid acquisition. Paid amplifies what works. It does not fix what's broken.

That's the build. Parts 3 through 9 are the data and the patterns. Read them with your niche pick from Step 1 in mind, and the index file open in another tab.

## Part 3: What 512 Real Whop Stores Tell Us

I scraped 512 stores. The titles and one-line descriptions are in the index file that ships with this playbook. Here's the meta-pattern from staring at all of them.

**Three niches own roughly 30% of the volume.** Trading (59 stores), Self-Improvement (54), and Niche/Hobby (44). Add Community/Mastermind (43) and Creator/Content (43) and you've covered just under half. Crypto/Web3 (29), AI Tools (25), Coding/Dev (25), Ecommerce (20), and Sports Betting (16) round out the top tier. The long tail (Real Estate, Course/Education, Tech/Dev, Copywriting, Adult/OF, Newsletter/Substack) sits between 2 and 10 stores each.

What this tells you: if you're launching in 2026, the saturated niches are saturated for a reason. They're where buyers spend money. The thin niches aren't underserved opportunities, they're underserved because the demand is genuinely smaller.

**Six monetization shapes cover almost every store.** I'll break them out in detail in Part 4, but the high-level taxonomy is: paid signals, paid community, paid course, paid newsletter, paid coaching cohort, and hybrid (course + community). Most stores collapse into one or two of those buckets. The hybrid shape is the highest-LTV pattern in the data.

**Pricing clusters at six anchors.** $19, $47-ish (call it $39 to $59), $97 (or $99), $197, $497, and $997+. The clustering is striking once you see it. There's almost nothing at $35, $145, or $720. People price at the anchors because those anchors are what the market has trained buyers to expect.

A few specific data points from the index:

- whop.com/source-trader (Day Trading With Source) tiers at $69.99/month and $17.99/month. Right at the $19 and $47 anchors.
- whop.com/wealth (Wealth SMMA) runs $7.99/month, $28.88/month, and a lifetime tier. They're explicitly anchoring low to capture impulse buyers.
- whop.com/solana-crypto-social-scanner runs $249.99/month. Premium signals, premium price.
- whop.com/n0ted (8 sessions/month coaching) sits at $98 for a 1-on-1 hour. Coaching anchor.

**Trading and Self-Improvement are not the same demographic.** They might look similar from outside (mostly young men, mostly aspirational), but the buying behavior is different. Trading buyers are looking for an edge they can deploy this week. Self-Improvement buyers are looking for a system they can join for months. Trading runs on signals and short cycles. Self-Improvement runs on community and identity. The pricing reflects that, with Self-Improvement skewing toward longer subscription terms and lower monthly prices.

**Community/Mastermind is the most format-flexible niche.** A mastermind on Whop can be a $19/month chat group or a $1,500/month inner circle. The 43 stores in this niche are spread across the entire price range. What unifies them is access to other people, not access to content.

**The Creator/Content niche is dominated by faceless YouTube and short-form clipping.** That's not what most people would predict, but the data is unambiguous. whop.com/faceless-kickstart, whop.com/facelessos, whop.com/wealthy-solopreneur, whop.com/short-form-clipping-program, whop.com/blackheart-clips, whop.com/clipforgeio. Faceless content is where the money is in Creator/Content right now. Anonymous content businesses are easier to scale because the "founder" doesn't have to be on camera, and the content doesn't run out when the founder gets bored.

**Real Estate and Copywriting are weirdly small.** 10 stores in Real Estate, 5 in Copywriting. These are huge industries with massive education markets, and yet they're under-indexed on Whop. My read: Real Estate buyers want professional certifications and physical-world deals, not Discord access. Copywriters live on Twitter and Skool, not Whop. If you're betting on those niches on Whop, you're betting against the data.

The full breakdown by niche is in Part 5. For now, the headline is: 6 shapes, 6 price anchors, 3 dominant niches, and a long tail of small but real markets.

## Part 4: The 6 Monetization Shapes Decoded

![The 6 Monetization Shapes comparison table covering price, cadence, format, LTV, and entry difficulty](embedded-visuals/embedded-2.jpg)

Most Whop stores collapse into one of six monetization shapes. Pick the shape that fits your offer, then steal pricing and packaging from the stores already running it.

### Shape 1: Paid Signals

**Where it lives:** Trading (dominant), Sports Betting (dominant), Crypto/Web3 (heavy).

**How it works:** The store sells access to a real-time alert feed. Trade calls, prop bets, memecoin scans. The buyer is paying for "what to do next," delivered hour-by-hour.

**Price range:** $19/month to $250/month. Sits squarely in the $19 to $97 cluster for most signals, with premium algos and prop-firm tools running higher.

**Cadence:** Continuous. The feed never stops during market hours. This is the highest-cadence shape in the data and the easiest to churn out of when the signals stop hitting.

**What subscribers actually get:** Trade alerts in Discord or Telegram, occasional voice/video commentary, a private chat to ask questions, sometimes a weekly recap.

**Real examples from the index:**
- whop.com/join-only-signals-subscription (Join Only Signals). exclusive trading signals across forex, metals, energy, and crypto
- whop.com/aibetscanner (AI Bet Scanner). daily sports picks driven by 3 algorithms, including straight bets and parlays
- whop.com/kick-s-picks (Kick's Picks +EV Plays). daily NBA player prop insights from a quantitative model
- whop.com/dip-bot-76 (Dip Bot). Solana memecoin signals, 24/7

**What they're doing right:** They keep the offer ruthlessly narrow. "NBA player props." "Solana memecoins." "+EV plays only." The narrower the niche, the easier it is for the buyer to evaluate whether the signals are working.

### Shape 2: Paid Community

**Where it lives:** Self-Improvement (dominant), Niche/Hobby (heavy), Community/Mastermind (definitionally).

**How it works:** Pay for access to a Discord or Telegram with the creator and other members. The product is the people. Content is secondary.

**Price range:** $19/month to $97/month for most stores. Premium masterminds push $200/month and up.

**Cadence:** Daily activity in chat, weekly creator interventions (live calls, AMAs), monthly themes or challenges.

**What makes people stay:** Identity. The community gives them a label they want to claim. "I'm in the Higher Brotherhood." "I'm in TRF." "I'm in the Discipline Academy." Pay-to-belong is a stronger retention mechanic than pay-to-learn.

**Real examples:**
- whop.com/the-higher-brotherhood (The Higher Brotherhood). community for young men working on themselves
- whop.com/trfbrotherhood (The Retention Formula). coaching and accountability
- whop.com/disciplinecircle (Discipline Circle). male community for structure and accountability
- whop.com/ai-frontrunners (AI Frontrunners) at whop.com/ai. community for AI tooling

**What they're doing right:** They sell the identity, not the content. The website copy talks about "brotherhood," "movement," "circle," "academy." Buyers join to become someone, not to consume something.

### Shape 3: Paid Course

**Where it lives:** Course/Education, AI Tools, Coding/Dev, Ecommerce, parts of Trading.

**How it works:** Pre-recorded lessons (video + PDF), accessed via the Whop interface. Often one-time priced, sometimes a flat subscription that gates the library.

**Price range:** $97 one-time to $497 one-time at the common end. Premium courses go to $997 or higher, especially in Trading and Ecommerce.

**Cadence:** Set-it-and-forget-it from the creator's side. The student watches at their own pace. Some stores ship occasional updates to keep the course feeling alive.

**Completion rate reality:** Low. Industry-wide, course completion sits around 5 to 15 percent. Most buyers buy out of intention, not out of follow-through. This is fine for the seller, fatal for the buyer's outcome.

**Real examples:**
- whop.com/aigrow (AI Ads Mastery Course). complete course on creating AI video ads
- whop.com/ai-master-me (AI Master Pro). 9+ hour course bundle on prompting and AI agents
- whop.com/price-action-beginner-course (Price Action BEGINNER Course). institutional concepts and trading strategies
- whop.com/amazon-fba-mastery-course-only (Amazon FBA Mastery). step-by-step Amazon seller training
- whop.com/aivideocreationbootcamp (AI Creator Bootcamp). viral content system

**What they're doing right:** The high-end courses (AI Creator Bootcamp, Ecommerce Mastery Camp at whop.com/ecommerce-mastery-camp) attach a Discord community to the course. The community is the retention mechanic. Without it, the course is just a YouTube playlist with a price tag.

### Shape 4: Paid Newsletter

**Where it lives:** Newsletter/Substack (definitionally), bits of Trading, bits of Crypto.

**How it works:** Paid email feed delivered weekly or daily, with optional Discord or community attached.

**Price range:** $19 to $97/month. Skews low because the email-only product has fewer perceived hours of value.

**Cadence:** Weekly is the dominant pattern. A Sunday or Monday delivery with market outlook, watchlist, or analysis.

**Why it's small on Whop:** Newsletters live on Substack and Beehiiv natively. Whop's strength is Discord and Telegram gating, not email. The newsletters that do appear on Whop are usually attached to a community or signals offer, not standalone.

**Real examples:**
- whop.com/premium-4f. newsletter editions every Sunday with market outlook and watchlist
- whop.com/rm-newsletter. free newsletter showcasing recent member wins

**What's working:** Both real examples here are tied to an existing trading or signals operation. The newsletter is a hook into the higher-priced product, not the product itself.

### Shape 5: Paid Coaching Cohort

**Where it lives:** Agency/SMMA (dominant), Self-Improvement (heavy), Trading (some).

**How it works:** A small-group coaching program with weekly live calls, a chat group, and direct access to the coach. Often runs 4, 8, or 12 weeks.

**Price range:** $497 to $5,000+ for a full cohort. The highest-priced shape on Whop.

**Cadence:** Weekly live calls. Daily chat. Cohorts of 10 to 50 typically, sometimes capped at 100.

**Tier pricing pattern:** Many Agency stores run 3 tiers. community-only at the bottom, group coaching in the middle, 1-on-1 access at the top. Wealth SMMA at whop.com/wealth runs this exactly: $7.99/month entry, $28.88/month monthly pass, and a lifetime tier.

**Real examples:**
- whop.com/agency-blueprint (Agency Blueprint). frameworks, SOPs, community access for marketing agency owners
- whop.com/eduro (Eduro Full Course Package). guide to launching, scaling, and mastering an SMMA
- whop.com/elite-coaching-academy (Elite Coaching Academy). coaching with a community framing
- whop.com/n0ted. 8 individual sessions a month, structured coaching program

**What they're doing right:** The high-end cohorts manufacture scarcity (cohort-based, capped seats, application-required). Scarcity raises the price ceiling. Cohorts that run continuously without a deadline tend to underprice themselves.

### Shape 6: Hybrid (Course + Community)

**Where it lives:** Everywhere. This is the highest-LTV shape in the data.

**How it works:** Pre-recorded course library plus an active Discord. The course is the entry hook, the community is the retention engine.

**Price range:** $97/month to $497/month subscription, or $497 to $1,997 one-time with a year of community.

**Why it works:** The course gets the buyer to commit. The community keeps them paying. Stores running this shape report retention curves 2 to 4x better than course-only or community-only.

**Real examples:**
- whop.com/agency-blueprint (Agency Blueprint). courses + 7-figure agency owners coaching + community
- whop.com/aivideocreationbootcamp (AI Creator Bootcamp). proven system + active community
- whop.com/contentacademy (Content Academy). frameworks + hand-on community involvement
- whop.com/futrgroup-access (FutrGroup AI Course). course + private group with active members
- whop.com/whop-academy-248e (Whop Academy). 5-day system to launch your first online offer plus ongoing access

**What they're doing right:** The pricing assumes the community has standalone value. The course alone justifies maybe half the price. The other half is the retention guarantee from being inside an active group.

## Part 5: The 20-Niche Map

![The 20-Niche Heatmap treemap showing all 512 Whop stores sized by niche store count](embedded-visuals/embedded-1.jpg)

For each niche, here's the count, the standout stores from the index, the typical price range, the buyer profile, and the cleanest entry play if you're starting in that niche.

### Trading (59 stores)

The biggest niche on Whop. Crowded, competitive, and full of paying buyers.

Standouts: whop.com/source-trader (Day Trading With Source, $69.99/month signals + $17.99/month alerts), whop.com/onlyoptionstrades (OnlyOptionsTrades, options community since 2020), whop.com/owls-options-traders (Owls Full Access, real trading floor), whop.com/the-prop-firm-protocol (The Prop Firm Protocol, system for passing prop firm phase 1), whop.com/stocktalk (Stock Talk Insiders, 50+ years combined experience).

Typical price: $19 to $97/month for signals, $197 to $497 for full courses, $997+ for prop-firm cohorts.

Buyer: 25 to 45-year-old man, has $5K to $50K trading capital, has been losing on his own and is willing to pay for a system. Wants live entries, not theory.

Entry play: Don't launch another generic trading signals group. Pick a single asset class (futures, options on SPY, prop firm payouts, memecoins) and own it.

### Self-Improvement (54 stores)

Second biggest niche. Tons of brotherhoods, discipline programs, dating, productivity.

Standouts: whop.com/the-higher-brotherhood (The Higher Brotherhood), whop.com/the-discipline-academy (The Discipline Academy), whop.com/disciplinecircle (Discipline Circle), whop.com/diegocastellanos (Raw Discipline Protocol), whop.com/be-together (The Modern Dating Blueprint).

Typical price: $19 to $97/month. Some premium coaching at $200+/month.

Buyer: 18 to 35-year-old man rebuilding from a low point. He's not paying for content, he's paying to belong somewhere structured.

Entry play: Identity-led. Pick a vivid identity ("the brotherhood," "the order," "the lab") and a concrete daily protocol. Don't sell theory.

### Niche / Hobby (44 stores)

The most fragmented niche. Cooking, gaming, music production, beauty, travel hacks, poker.

Standouts: whop.com/seanperrywins (Sean Perry Wins, world #3 ranked poker player), whop.com/innovatech-solutions-inc-b8c8 (Travel Hacks 26.0), whop.com/softpluse (FL Studio 24 Lifetime), whop.com/zylohub (gaming community).

Typical price: $19 to $47 one-time or $9 to $29/month.

Buyer: hobbyist, has spending money for the hobby, wants insider knowledge or a community of fellow obsessives.

Entry play: Tiny audience, deep commitment. If you have 1,000 true fans of an obscure hobby, you can monetize them at $20/month and clear $20K/month. Don't try to scale beyond your competence in the hobby.

### Community / Mastermind (43 stores)

Format-flexible. Some are $9 chat groups, some are $1,500/month inner circles.

Standouts: whop.com/c (The TradingHub Community Free), whop.com/1-recession-proof-mastermind (1% Recession Proof Mastermind), whop.com/community-3-0 (Community 3.0), whop.com/waitlist (The Mastermind, e-commerce focus), whop.com/kctrades (KC Trades Mastermind).

Typical price: $19 to $1,500/month, with most clustering at $97 to $297/month.

Buyer: operator-level, wants peer access more than content.

Entry play: You need credibility before you launch a mastermind. Run the cohort offer first, prove the outcome, then graduate to a mastermind tier. Cold mastermind launches die.

### Creator / Content (43 stores)

Dominated by faceless YouTube and short-form clipping.

Standouts: whop.com/faceless-kickstart (The Faceless Kickstart), whop.com/facelessos (Faceless OS, 4,000+ scripts), whop.com/wealthy-solopreneur (Faceless Wealth Starter Kit), whop.com/short-form-clipping-program, whop.com/blackheart-clips, whop.com/tubebuilders (Your First 30 YouTube Videos).

Typical price: $97 to $497 one-time for courses; $19 to $97/month for clipping communities.

Buyer: 19 to 30-year-old looking for a side income that doesn't require being on camera or starting a real business.

Entry play: Niche down inside faceless content. "Faceless YouTube for sleep stories." "Faceless TikTok for dropshipping ad creatives." Generic faceless YouTube is saturated.

### Fitness (35 stores)

Surprisingly fragmented, with a heavy weight-loss skew.

Standouts: whop.com/the-vitality-codex (Strength Training Blueprint), whop.com/lqf (Olive Grove Society, Mediterranean wellness for women), whop.com/mattsmethod (The Method, fat loss + muscle preservation), whop.com/el1te-man (THE HIGH-EFFICIENCY REVOLUTION), whop.com/way-of-life-nutrition (Nutrition handouts library).

Typical price: $19 to $97/month coaching, $97 to $297 one-time for programs.

Buyer: 30 to 50-year-old who's tried 3 to 5 fitness programs and bounced. Wants accountability more than another PDF.

Entry play: Specific demographic (men 40+, women postpartum, Mediterranean cooks, calisthenics-only) wins over generic fitness coaching.

## Part 6: The "How to Copy a Winning Pattern" Framework

![The 5-step Pattern-Copy Framework flow from promise to trust signal with a worked prop firm example](embedded-visuals/embedded-4.jpg)

You don't need to invent a new monetization shape. The 6 shapes in Part 4 cover everything that works. What you need is to study a successful Whop store and extract its pattern, then redeploy it in your niche. Here's the 5-step framework.

### Step 1: Identify the Promise

Read the store's headline. What's the one-line outcome the buyer is paying for?

Examples from the data:

- whop.com/aibetscanner. "daily sports picks and prop predictions driven by 3 algorithms"
- whop.com/the-prop-firm-protocol. "stop failing phase 1, the internal system to pass prop firm challenges"
- whop.com/the-higher-brotherhood. "a community of friendly and determined young men who strive to become the very best version of themselves"
- whop.com/agency-blueprint. "the exact frameworks, SOPs, and strategies to land high-ticket clients, build your team, and scale your marketing agency"

The promise is the starting point. Strip away the brand, the design, the pricing. What's the outcome being sold?

### Step 2: Identify the Format

Which of the 6 shapes is this? Sometimes it's clean (signals, course). Sometimes it's hybrid. Pin down the exact format before you copy anything.

For a hybrid offer, decompose. "It's a 12-module course plus a Discord plus a weekly live call." Now you know what you'd need to build to match it.

### Step 3: Identify the Cadence

How often does the buyer get something? Daily signal? Weekly call? On-demand course access? Monthly themed challenge?

The cadence dictates the price defensibility. A daily signal can charge more per month than a once-a-month update because the buyer gets reinforcement every day. A course-only offer at $97/month feels expensive after month 2 because the cadence is zero.

### Step 4: Identify the Price Anchor

What's this priced against? "Cheaper than a coach" ($97 vs $500). "Cheaper than the loss" (sports picks at $30/month vs losing $500 on a bet). "Cheaper than a course in this niche" ($197 instead of the $997 industry standard).

The price anchor explains why the price feels reasonable. Without an anchor, the same price feels arbitrary.

### Step 5: Identify the Trust Signal

What proves they can deliver? Common patterns from the data:

- Specific performance claims (whop.com/@stakeheads: "2024-2025 NFL RECORD 256-0-213")
- Years of experience (whop.com/source-trader: "9-year pro")
- External credibility (whop.com/alexcaruso: "Featured on ESPN")
- Volume of work (whop.com/internet-money: "$400,000+/month on Google Ads spend")
- Community size or testimonials (whop.com/etsymafia testimonials throughout)
- Founder track record (whop.com/seanperrywins: "#3 Ranked Poker Player in World 2021 with over $7 million+ in Winnings")

Pick the strongest available trust signal and lead the page with it. If you don't have one yet, build it before you launch (run the offer for cheap or free, get 5 case studies, then raise the price).

### Worked Example

Let me run the framework on whop.com/the-prop-firm-protocol (The Prop Firm Protocol).

- **Promise:** Pass prop firm phase 1 instead of failing it.
- **Format:** Course (a "system") plus implied community.
- **Cadence:** Course is on-demand, but the implication is ongoing learning.
- **Price anchor:** Cheaper than the cost of failing prop firm challenges (typically $200 to $1,000 each in entry fees lost).
- **Trust signal:** Frames the founder as having an internal system, which signals proprietary knowledge.

Now I want to launch a copy in a different niche. Say, "Pass the day-trading-prop-firm phase 2." Same format, same shape, different niche specificity. The framework gives me the bones. My job is to fill them with my own credibility and my own specifics.

Run this framework on 5 to 10 stores in your target niche. The pattern that repeats is the pattern you should copy.

## Part 7: Pricing Patterns Across the 512 Stores

![Pricing Distribution chart showing the 4 price zones and the share of stores clustered in each](embedded-visuals/embedded-3.jpg)

Pricing on Whop clusters in 4 zones. Each zone serves a different buyer mindset.

### The $19 Starter Zone ($9 to $29)

This is the impulse-buy tier. Buyer is testing the offer, hasn't committed to a relationship, wants to see if the chat is active.

Where it lives: low-trust niches where buyers churn fast. Sports betting daily passes. Signal groups. Beginner crypto communities. Free-to-low-paid funnels.

Real examples:
- whop.com/sports-betting-01-cfb8. $25/month sports parlays
- whop.com/wealth. $7.99/month entry tier
- whop.com/source-trader. $17.99/month stock alerts

Why it works: low risk to buyer, buyer is essentially auditioning the operator.

Risk: high churn. Operators in this zone need volume to make the math work, which means paid ads, affiliates, and content marketing.

### The $47 to $97 Commitment Zone

This is where the bulk of Whop pricing sits. Buyer has decided the offer is worth real money but isn't committing to premium.

Where it lives: most paid communities, most signals groups beyond the entry tier, most courses, most coaching at the bottom of the ladder.

Real examples:
- whop.com/source-trader. $69.99/month for the Source Day Trading core
- whop.com/n0ted. $98 for a 1-on-1 coaching hour
- whop.com/nicklifts and whop.com/sickboytrades. community access in this zone

Why it works: the buyer feels meaningful skin in the game, but the commitment is small enough to renew without thinking too hard.

Risk: this is the most crowded zone, so differentiation matters more than pricing.

### The $197 to $497 Outcome Zone

This is where the buyer is paying for a specific outcome. Premium signals, full-length courses, group coaching, and high-end masterminds.

Where it lives: course-heavy stores, agency offers, premium signals.

Real examples:
- whop.com/solana-crypto-social-scanner. $249.99/month
- whop.com/agency-blueprint and similar agency offers price in this band
- Many of the courses in Trading and Ecommerce land at $297 to $497 one-time

Why it works: the price signals seriousness, and serious buyers self-select.

Risk: needs strong proof. Generic positioning at this price gets crushed.

### The $997+ Premium Zone

Small-cohort masterminds, full agency programs, and 1-on-1 coaching.

Where it lives: high-trust offers in Agency, Trading, and Self-Improvement. Often run as cohorts with 10 to 50 seats.

Real examples are sparser in the public data because high-end offers tend to gate pricing behind applications, but stores like whop.com/agency-blueprint and Eduro at whop.com/eduro signal high-end pricing in their copy structure.

Why it works: the price IS the marketing. A $2,500/month cohort attracts buyers who would never pay $97.

Risk: needs an existing audience. Cold launches at this price almost always fail.

### The Free Tier as Funnel Pattern

Almost every successful operator runs a free Whop as a funnel into the paid product. The free Whop is a low-friction discovery surface, an audience-builder, and a credibility play.

Examples:
- whop.com/c (The TradingHub Community Free)
- whop.com/the-crypto-world-free (The Crypto World Free)
- whop.com/trading-university-ac (EmmanuelTrades Free Course)
- whop.com/free-forex-trading-course-free-signals (Forex Trading Course & Signals)
- whop.com/wiredd (Wired Free Course)

The pattern: free Whop captures email + Discord access. Then upsell to the paid tier via in-Discord pitches, weekly live calls, and email sequences.

If you're launching, your free tier should not be your real product. It should be the smallest possible piece of value that demonstrates competence and gets the buyer into your audience.

## Part 9: The 60-Day Whop Store Launch Plan (Compact Calendar)

Part 2 walked through the full build sequence step by step. This is the same work mapped onto a 60-day calendar so you can see the timeline at a glance and check yourself against the milestones.

### Weeks 1-2: Niche Selection + Offer Spec

Run the 5-question filter (Part 2 Step 1) against your candidate niches. Pull the 10 nearest competitors from the index file in your chosen niche. Run the pattern-copy framework (Part 6) on each. Draft your one-page offer spec: promise, format, cadence, price, trust signal.

KPI: one-page offer spec written, reviewed by at least one peer.

### Weeks 3-4: Build the Whop Store + First Content

Stand up the store, brand kit, about page, and the 3-tier structure (Part 2 Step 3). Pre-load the first 4 weeks of content. If you're running signals, draft 20 example signals. If you're running a course, record the first 4 modules. If you're running a community, write the first 4 weeks of weekly themes. Set up the welcome flow (DM, intro thread, week-1 starter kit).

KPI: store live, Discord live, first 4 weeks of content loaded.

### Weeks 5-6: First 50 Members (Organic)

Run the warm launch playbook from Part 2 Step 6. Existing audience first. Free first 10 in exchange for testimonials. Founding-member 50% off for members 11 through 25. 50 personal DMs. Ask testimonials within 7 days.

KPI: 50 paying members, 5 testimonials, first month of content delivered to schedule.

### Weeks 7-8: Pricing Iteration + Retention Systems

Test one price increase to the next anchor up (e.g., $19 to $47, or $97 to $197). Most operators underprice on launch and discover by week 6 that buyers would have paid more. Lock in the 30/60/90 retention cadence from Part 2 Step 7. Cancel or refund disengaged members at the 30-day mark.

KPI: 70% month-2 retention or better, price tested at least once, retention cadence running on autopilot.

### Day 60: Honest Self-Audit

By day 60 you should have 50 to 100 paying members, $2,000 to $10,000 in MRR, a working offer with a clear retention story, 5 to 15 testimonials, and a clear answer on whether to scale or pivot.

If you're below 50 at day 60, the offer is the problem, not the marketing. Re-run the Part 6 framework on your own offer. Find which element (promise, format, cadence, price, trust signal) is the broken one.

If you're above 100 at day 60, you have a real business. The work shifts from acquisition to retention.
